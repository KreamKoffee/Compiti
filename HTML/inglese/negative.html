Negative Effects

1. Increase in Average Temperature:

Consequences for Human Health: More intense heatwaves can lead to severe health issues, with an increase in cases of heat strokes and heat-related illnesses. Impacts on Ecosystems: Ecosystems are influenced by temperature variations, potentially leading to biodiversity loss and disruptions in natural cycles.

2. Melting of Glaciers and Polar Ice Caps:

Rising Sea Levels: The melting of glaciers and polar ice caps contributes to rising sea levels, threatening coastal communities and increasing the risk of flooding.

3. Rise in Sea Levels:

Threat to Coastal Communities: Coastal areas and low-lying islands are particularly vulnerable to rising sea levels, with potential consequences for the security and stability of local communities.

4. Changes in Weather Patterns:

Extreme Weather Events: Global warming can intensify extreme weather events, leading to more frequent floods, prolonged droughts, more powerful hurricanes, and increasingly widespread wildfires.
